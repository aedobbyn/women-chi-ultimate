Intro

Thank you for taking the time to complete the Women in Chicago Ultimate 2016 Survey! This survey grew out of a desire from women at all levels of the Chicago ultimate scene to come together and understand how we can strengthen our community. The plan is to get a lot of women to take this survey, then we can come together to discuss the results. Your responses will help us come up with innovative ways to improve and strengthen the quality of women's experiences in Chicago ultimate at all levels.

This survey should take 15-20 minutes. Thank you in advance for taking it, and please feel free to reach out to Steph Landry (steph(dot)landry0(at)gmail(dot)com) with any questions!

DESCRIPTION: This series of questions asks for your opinion on the biggest barriers and threats to recruiting and retaining women in ultimate at all levels.

What are the biggest barriers in recruiting & retaining NEW women to play ultimate?

  • Lack of instructional leagues
  • Lack of instruction on individual teams (e.g. captains/leaders do not support development of new female players)
  • Lack of female captains
  • Too few social opportunities to go along with playing opportunities
  • Lack of direct outreach and recruitment of new female players
  • New women feeling like they are “not good enough” to play a certain league/team
  • No “next step” opportunities (e.g. no formal opportunities to learn beyond the basics)
  • Negative first playing experiences that cause women to not continue playing
  • Frustration over lack of inclusion during play (e.g. women at various levels not being thrown to during games)
  • Logistical concerns: playing opportunities don't fit new players' schedules/locations
  • Injury
  • Family/career
  • Time commitment required

What are the biggest threats to retaining EXPERIENCED women in ultimate?

  • Lack of opportunity for continued competitive play
  • Too few social opportunities to go along with playing opportunities
  • General negative/frustrating playing experiences in leagues
  • General negative/frustrating playing experiences on club teams
  • Negative/frustrating playing experiences in leagues/club teams that SPECIFICALLY relate to gender inequity (e.g. always getting looked off by men on league team)
  • Injury
  • Feeling burnt out from playing or coordinating too much ultimate
  • Not enough opportunity to play at the desired level
  • Logistical concerns: playing opportunities don’t fit schedules/locations
  • Family/career concenrs
